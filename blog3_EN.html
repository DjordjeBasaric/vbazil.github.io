Do dogs love dog cages?

Nikola Petrovic

Dogs are by nature animals that look for a lair, a smaller fenced corner where they will feel safe. This instinctive need can be easily noticed in dogs living on the streets. You may also notice this need in your pet, especially if the dog is afraid of noise, such as thunder and firecrackers. If you notice that the dog is retreating to a certain part of the apartment, e.g. under the table, it also demonstrates to you the natural need for zajbin.

Dog cages are an ideal solution as a replacement for a lair. Not only can it be a safe place for your pet, a dog cage can also be the shape of a dog house indoors and a place where your pet will be able to rest without interruption.

In addition to the collar and leash, the dog cage is a proven effective tool in training, recommended by many trainers, breeders and veterinarians. Once you get used to the cage, unwanted dog behavior can be completely avoided. These include defecating in the apartment, destroying things, digging on the parquet, unnecessary barking, as well as howling. The dog cage is also suitable for overcoming separation anxiety, which is particularly prone to certain breeds. It is easier to transport a dog that is used to a dog cage, which contributes to safer driving for both the dog and the driver.

A dog cage as a means of teaching a dog where to defecate

A dog cage is proven to be the fastest way to teach a dog to defecate outside. Instinctively, dogs do not defecate where they sleep. Dogs naturally avoid staying near the place where they defecate. When the guard is taken out immediately, after being released from the cage, he will quickly learn what is expected of him. It is very important that the dog is run regularly, especially when it comes to puppies that should be run outside every hour, and especially immediately after play and meals.
