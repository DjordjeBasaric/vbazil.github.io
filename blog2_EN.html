How to choose the ideal scratcher for a cat?

Petar Nikolic

If you've ever wondered "How to choose the ideal cat scratcher?" the one that won’t end up as another dust-collecting item, in the text that follows you’ll get the answer. Choosing the ideal cat scratcher can be a frustrating and expensive endeavor. It can happen that, over time, you buy more scrapers and that your pet ignores all but one, except for his favorite - your couch. The conclusion may be that your cat does not like scratchers, which is rare.

People often make the mistake of choosing a scraper that they like, instead of putting their pet in the foreground and thinking about what he will like. It takes a little knowledge to choose the ideal pet scratcher, which will be of more interest to cats than a couch in the room.

Cats prefer high scratches

The cat scratcher should be high enough so that the cat can fully stretch while scratching. This stretch contributes to the health and flexibility of your pet. If the cat needs to squat during the scratching, it will not get the full benefit from exercising and stretching the muscles, and this position can be quite uncomfortable for it. A cat may occasionally prefer to squat while scratching, but it is crucial to be given a choice and there is an option to be able to stretch completely. Buying a cat scraper that is taller than 80 cm is definitely a good starting choice.

What is the best scratching material?

Cat scratchers are made of different materials. Many are designed to be attractive to humans, putting the needs and desires of cats in the background. Cats prefer solid material that crumbles under their claws. If the scratching is accompanied by a loud sound, the cat is happier because of that. Avoid carpet scratchers for cats. Although cats may like these scratchers, the problem is that the same material is in your home, and you certainly don't want carpets around the apartment to turn into cat scratchers. You can’t expect a cat to make a difference as to why it’s good to scratch the carpet found on the scratcher, and it’s not good to do the same with the carpet found on the floor of your home. In addition, carpet scrapers can occasionally pin your pet's claws, which can lead to injury.
