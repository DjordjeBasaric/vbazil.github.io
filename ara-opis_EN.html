Ara Rara

The macaw is a species of large colorful parrot from America. They are the largest birds in the parrot family in terms of height and wingspan, although the cacao is heavier. There are 18 species of macaws. Ara Their natural habitat is forests, especially the rainforests of Mexico and Central and South America. They move in noisy flocks, except during mating. They feed on fruit, seeds and Brazil nuts. The largest is the red-blue (scarlet) macaw. It is 90 centimeters long. It is scarlet red on the abdomen. It has light blue wings and back and a red tail. The blue-yellow macaw is also known. She has a blue head and back, and a yellow lower body. Are are frequent pets. They like to talk and sing, and they can also train. Unfortunately, their shiny feathers and social lifestyle make them easy prey for hunters, so several species of macaws became extinct in the Caribbean.
